I am a final-year undergraduate student at the Indian Institute of Techology, Madras where I'm pursuing my majors in Data Science and Chemical Engineering. As part of my final year thesis, I am working with Dr. Sutanu Chakraborti at the AIDB Lab and Dr. Deepak P, Queen's University Belfast, UK on explainablibility and fairness in non-parametric models. I am primarily interested in making AI more explainable so that sound decisions can be made in engineering and scientific applications, with the goal of achieving the interpretability provided by first principles approaches while vastly improving the performance.

Previously, I've been awarded the MITACS Globalink Scholarship and have worked with Dr. Paul Ayers at McMaster University, Canada, on diverse subset selection to train models for effective prediction of drug efficacy. I wrote my bachelor's thesis with the Process Systems Engineering and Data Science Lab supervised by Dr. Arun Tangirala. I have also interned at Goldman Sachs as a summer analyst in their Model Risk Management Team. Additionally, I have been a student researcher at the Robert Bosch Centre for Data Science and Artificial Intelligence guided by Dr. Raghunathan Rengaswamy where we showed the potency of neural networks in proposing new quinone derivatives, to make flow batteries more effective, while respecting fundamental chemistry principles.

    		• Causal Discovery in Natural Language Text

    		Caching in Deep Learning Architectures for Robustness

    [report]

    We proposed a cache-like framework on existing models such as DenseNet, MobileNet, etc. to accelerate their inference time, while maintaining an accuracy close to their original. The triplet loss function was used to retrain a segment of these neural networks and obtain similar hidden output vectors for similar inputs. Further, a shallow neural network called ProtoNN was implemented to emulate kNN on the modified hidden layer outputs. In doing so, we reduced the inference time of DenseNet-121 by 23% maintaining top-1 accuracy at 74.32% (original accuracy: 76.58%. This was also seen to improve robustness to adverserial attacks.
    		Reliably Predicting Solubility of Quinone Derivatives for Flow Batteries

    Under submission [paper]

    We conceptualised a robust model to estimate the solvation free energy of quinone derivatives. Further, we demonstrated that the model obeyed fundamental organic chemistry principles without having explicity been taught to do so. This allowed us to identify novel Quinone derivatives to improve battery performance, significantly improving on literature.

    		Graph Algorithms for Kidney Exchange

    Selected for GsSOC '21 [report][code]

    In this work, we built an Integer Linear Programming model to facilitate kidney transplants among patient-donor pairs having an incompatible but willing family donor. Our system improves upon the system currently prevalent in India, by incorporating new parameters to maximise the likelihood of a successful match. Further, we designed a stable platform for hospitals to automate the matching proces.
    		Counterfactuals as Explanations for Monotonic Classifiers

    Accepted at ICCBR XCBR Workshop '22 [paper][slides]

    We developed a novel explanation algorithm to generate actionable counterfactuals incorporating domain knowledge using monotonic constraints. The generated counterfactuals were 10% closer to the queries as compared to the baseline, with 70% more coverage.
    		Estimation of Overlap among Interdisciplinary Courses

    [report]

    In this project we estimated the extent of overlap among interdisciplinary courses across the departments at IIT Madras. We identified overlapping concepts using phrase extraction techniques and highlighted the overlapping concepts for enhanced explainability. We obtained lists of overlapping courses for 100+ cases. In doing so, we improved the average nDCG@10 by 5% over the baseline.
